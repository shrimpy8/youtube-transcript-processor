# Key Insights & Takeaways

-   **Speed and Efficiency**: AI drastically accelerates traditional PM tasks; market research can be done in minutes, and PRD generation in seconds, enabling PMs to move from idea to prototype at unprecedented speeds.

# AI Tools & Workflows

## Perplexity
-   **How it's used**: For rapid market research and user sentiment analysis. It leverages a "discussions and opinions" filter to search platforms like Reddit for real-world user opinions and concerns.
-   **Workflow**:
    1.  Ask a question (e.g., "Would families be interested in a smart fridge?").
    2.  Prompt Perplexity to "Create two items: one that is pro smart fridge and one that is against smart fridge. Use everything you read and have these two agents debate at least... 20 times about it and give me the minimum set of features I would need in order to convince the against agent."
-   **Key tip**: Prompting the AI to create opposing personas (pro/con agents) helps generate more critical thinking and identify the minimum features needed for product-market fit, avoiding the AI's tendency to be agreeable.

## Custom GPT (AI Product GBD)
-   **How it's used**: To generate Product Requirements Documents (PRDs) quickly and in a specific style/voice.
-   **Workflow**: Input the minimum set of features identified from Perplexity's debate into the custom GPT with a prompt like "generate a PRD about a smart fridge that has these features."
-   **Key tip**: Pre-training the GPT with a template of your favorite PRD ensures outputs are in your desired style, providing an "incredible head start" and allowing more time for strategic thinking.

## Vzero (and similar tools like Google AI Studio, Lovable)
-   **How it's used**: For rapid UI prototyping from a PRD.
-   **Workflow**: Copy the generated PRD from the custom GPT and paste it into Vzero with the prompt "create the UI of a smart fridge given this PRD as an input."
-   **Key tip**: Using a high-quality, structured PRD as input significantly improves the quality of the prototype, reducing the need for later tweaks. The prototype serves as a "communication tool" for product reviews, allowing stakeholders to "see my vision having flesh" and "touch it, feel it, see it, move around."

## Flow (Google Labs) / Veo / Sora
-   **How it's used**: For text-to-video generation to create promotional clips, visualize user experiences, and influence stakeholders.
-   **Workflow**: Prompt with a description or features, e.g., "create a promotional clip for a couple using a smart fridge that has these features."
-   **Key tip**: The host suggests using a **narrative** (a story about an end-user gaining value from the product) from the PRD as a prompt for video generation, rather than just a list of features, to ensure a more user-centric and less feature-over-indexed output.
-   **Sora features**: Allows creation of "cameos" (AI avatars) with custom instructions (e.g., "make me have a pet pegasus with me at all times").

## Notebook LM
-   **How it's used**: To leverage AI as a judge or evaluator for demo days, hackathons, or pitch competitions.
-   **Workflow**:
    1.  Record and upload audio files of each pitch/presentation.
    2.  Add specific instructions and criteria for the AI judge (e.g., "select the top three apps based on these criteria: innovation, impact, and storytelling").
    3.  Generate an audio overview or an interactive podcast-style announcement of the winners.
-   **Key tip**: This tool can provide "third-party analysis" and makes evaluations engaging, with AI hosts interacting dynamically with user prompts.
